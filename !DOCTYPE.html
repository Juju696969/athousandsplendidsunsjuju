A Story of Unbreakable Bonds

Set against the volatile events of Afghanistan's tumultuous history, from the Soviet invasion to the reign of the Taliban to post-Taliban rebuilding

The Heart of Afghanistan

Through the lives of Mariam and Laila, two women born a generation apart, Hosseini creates a rich tapestry of family and friendship, of mothers and daughters, in a land torn by war, rocked by social upheaval, and scarred by the repression of women.

This is a story of resilience, of quiet heroism, and of the extraordinary lengths to which ordinary people will go to protect the ones they love.

Emotional Journey

A deeply moving exploration of love, sacrifice, and the unbreakable bonds between mothers and daughters, wives and husbands.

Historical Depth

Set against thirty years of Afghan history, from the Soviet occupation through the rise and fall of the Taliban.

Universal Themes

Themes of friendship, resilience, and hope that resonate across cultures and generations, making it a truly universal story.

"A Story That Will Stay With You Forever"

Hosseini masterfully weaves together the stories of Mariam, a young woman whose life is shaped by the harsh realities of her time, and Laila, born into a more hopeful world that is quickly shattered by war. Their unlikely friendship becomes a testament to the enduring power of female solidarity and the lengths to which people will go to protect those they love.

With stunning prose and unforgettable characters, "A Thousand Splendid Suns" illuminates the grace, resilience, and quiet heroism of Afghan women, creating a portrait that is both intimate and sweeping in its historical scope.

About Khaled Hosseini

Master storyteller and humanitarian, bringing Afghan stories to the world

A Voice for Afghanistan

Khaled Hosseini was born in Kabul, Afghanistan, in 1965. His family moved to the United States in 1980, and he became a citizen in 1989. He graduated from college in 1988 and earned a medical degree in 1993. He practiced medicine for over ten years, until a year and a half after the publication of The Kite Runner.

Hosseini is also a Goodwill Envoy to the UN Refugee Agency, UNHCR, and the founder of The Khaled Hosseini Foundation, a nonprofit organization which provides humanitarian assistance to the people of Afghanistan.
